Tricia Ngoon



I am a fourth-year Ph.D student in Cognitive Science at UCSD. I work with Dr. Scott Klemmer and collaborate with Dr. Caren Walker. My research focuses on scaffolding creativity and problem-solving in education.


I graduated from UC Berkeley with a degree in Psychology and previously worked at the Stanford Cognitive & Systems Neuroscience Lab.


Projects


Peer Feedback

Through two deployments and two controlled experiments with a novel feedback system, CritiqueKit, we demonstrate that suggestions and interactive guidance help novices provide specific, actionable, and justified feedback.

Creative Education

We hypothesize that metacognitive scaffolding techniques can help learners better monitor and improve their creative learning and skills.

Patterning

Preschool children often have difficulty abstracting their patterning knowledge to new patterns or contexts. We hypothesize that giving preschoolers a variety of pattern learning epxeriences will help them perform better at new patterning tasks.

Contextual Assistance

Creative tools are often highly complex and difficult for novices to learn. We developed RePlay, an application-agnostic Mac OS panel that presents tutorial videos to users in their own context.
